Career Pathways Exploration

  1. Finance & Accounting:

    Careers in Chartered Accountancy (CA), Cost and Management Accountancy (CMA), Financial Analysis, Investment Banking, and Auditing.

  2. Business & Management:

    Explore roles in Business Administration, Entrepreneurship, Operations Management, and Marketing.

  3. Economics & Policy:

    Consider careers in Economic Analysis, Policy Research, International Trade, and Consulting.

  4. Corporate Law:

    Opportunities in Corporate Law, Legal Consultancy, and Compliance within businesses.

  5. Banking & Financial Services:

    ositions in Commercial Banking, Corporate Finance, Financial Planning, or Wealth Management.

Skill Development

  1. Numerical & Analytical Skills:

    Build proficiency in Mathematics, Accounting, and Economics to excel in fields like finance and economics.

  2. Business Acumen:

    Develop strong understanding in business principles, management practices, and entrepreneurship through case studies, internships, and real-world projects

  3. Communication & Negotiation

    Enhance verbal and written communication skills, crucial for careers in law, management, and marketing.

  4. Digital & Financial Tools:

    Learn to use tools like Microsoft Excel, SAP, Tally, QuickBooks, and analytics platforms for data-driven decision-making.

Program Selection

  • BCom (Bachelor of Commerce):

    Core programs in Accounting, Finance, Economics, and Business Studies.

  • BBA (Bachelor of Business Administration):

    Specialize in Marketing, Finance, International Business, or HR.

  • BA in Economics:

    Focuses on Economic Theory, Policy, and Financial Economics for analytical careers.

  • BMS (Bachelor of Management Studies):

    For students interested in operations, supply chain, or business development.

  • BBA LLB (Law):

    Integrated 5-year program for careers in Corporate Law, Business Law, and Legal Consultancy.

Roadmap For Advancement

  • Undergraduate Degree:

    Select your focus from BCom, BBA, BMS, or Economics to set the foundation for further specialization.

  • Professional Certifications:

    Pursue CA, CMA, CFA (Chartered Financial Analyst), CFP (Certified Financial Planner), or ACCA for specialization in Finance.

  • Postgraduate Specialzation::

    Consider pursuing MBA, MA in Economics, or LLM for advanced studies in management, economics, or law.

  • Entrepreneurship & Startups:

    For students inclined towards entrepreneurship, focus on building business skills and network through incubators and startup programs.
